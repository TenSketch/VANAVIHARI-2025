🌊 JALATARANGINI WATERFALL

📍Location:

📝 Description

At a distance of 7 km from Maredumilli Bus Stand, Jalatharangini is a picturesque waterfall situated on the Rajahmundry-Bhadrachalam Highway in Maredumilli village, East Godavari district, Andhra Pradesh. It is a seasonal waterfall and also one of the must-visit places in Maredumilli.

Jalatharangini Waterfalls is a cascade of running water amidst the lush forest and rocky terrain. This is a multi-tier waterfall. During monsoon season, this small waterfall is full of gush and would be a heaven for nature lovers. The water of the fall is crystal clear.

One needs to walk 150 m to reach the falls from the entry point, which takes 30 minutes. The trekking path is slippery and a little strenuous. The waterfall is maintained by local tribes, and they maintain the area by making steps and preparing an easy jungle path with side barricade supports. There is ample parking space for vehicles.

💧 AMRUTHADHARA WATERFALL

📍 Location:
📝 Description

At a distance of 15 km from Maredumilli Bus Stand, Amruthadhara is a beautiful waterfall situated on the Rajahmundry–Bhadrachalam Highway near Maredumilli in Alluri Sitarama Raju district of Andhra Pradesh. It is a perennial waterfall and one of the must-visit places in Maredumilli.

Amruthadhara is formed over a seasonal stream amidst forest and steep rocks. During monsoon, this small waterfall is in full gush and would be a heaven for nature lovers. The waterfall is in two stages and is not comfortable for bathing.

Visitors need to climb down a steep 1 km pathway from the entrance to reach the waterfalls, which takes about 1 hour. The trekking path is slippery and strenuous. The waterfall is maintained by local tribes, who have made steps and prepared an easy jungle path with side barricade supports. There is ample space for vehicle parking.

🏞️ KARTHIKAVANAM PICNIC SPOT

📍 Location:
📝 Description

At a distance of 10 km from Maredumilli Bus Stand, Karthikavanam is a beautiful picnic spot situated on the Rajahmundry–Bhadrachalam Highway near Maredumilli in Alluri Sitarama Raju district of Andhra Pradesh.

The Forest Department has developed a scenic wildlife viewing area here, known as Karthikavanam, featuring tropical viewpoints, golden bamboo groves, and pine trees along the Pamuleru River. The place is frequented by wild animals like tiger, panther, bison, peacocks, red jungle fowl, and other bird species, along with a variety of butterflies.

🌿 MEDICINAL PLANTS CONSERVATION AREA (MPCA) PICNIC SPOT

📍 Location:
📝 Description

At a distance of 4 km from Maredumilli Bus Stand, the Medicinal Plants Conservation Area (MPCA) is a beautiful botanical preservation site situated along the Rajahmundry–Bhadrachalam Highway near Maredumilli in Alluri Sitarama Raju district of Andhra Pradesh.

The flora here was identified using the belt transect method. So far, 203 species have been identified, recorded, and documented. Out of these, 83 species are in the RET (Rare, Endangered, and Threatened) list, and 3 are endemic species unique to the region.

⛰️ GUDISA VIEW POINT

📍 Location:
📝 Description

Gudisa View Point, nestled in the Gudisa Hill range of Andhra Pradesh, offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Eastern Ghats. Famous for its sweeping grasslands, lush valleys, dense forests, and mist-covered hills, it is a paradise for nature lovers, trekkers, and photographers.

The journey includes a challenging 12 km stretch along the Gudisa Ghat Road, with sharp bends and unpaved paths, leading to open grasslands atop hills over 1,235 meters in altitude. These unique high-altitude ecosystems feature sparse, stunted vegetation, semi-evergreen forests, and rich biodiversity, including several Rare, Endangered, and Threatened (RET) species—three of which are found only here. Streams meandering through rocky terrains add to the charm, especially during sunrise and sunset.

Located within the Maredumilli Range of the Rampachodavaram Division Reserve Forests, Gudisa Hills is also home to the Konda Reddy tribe. Visitors can explore preserved tribal arts, crafts, and traditional knowledge, along with guided tours covering trekking trails, birdwatching spots (including the rare Green Munia), and nature education programs.

Tourism here is regulated to protect the fragile ecosystem—plastic is banned, overnight stays are prohibited, and sustainable practices are encouraged to ensure its beauty endures for generations.
